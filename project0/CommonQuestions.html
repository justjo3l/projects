Common Questions

What is EDM?

EDM simply stands for Electronic Dance Music. It is the music genre that involves man-made synthesizers and softwares. It is also often called Dance Music or Club Music because it's the kind of music that's usually played in festivals, clubs and other party-like events.

How is EDM different from other genres?

EDM is the music genre with the most sub-genres. It has over 160 subgenres and is still increasing. It involves creative thinking of the artists and producers. The specialty of EDM is often thought to be the eye to detail and perfection of the songs they make. It also does not require a natural skill in singing, but rather just requires a love for music and creativity.

Is EDM considered real music?

Often audiences think that EDM requires little to no effort or creativity and hence cannot be considered music. To be honest, This could not be any further from the truth. A simple EDM song goes through various different stages before it is completed. Some such stages are Melody-Making, Mixing, Mastering and many more. The entire process of making these songs can range from a week or two to a year or two. It is also worth noting that a person who makes EDM music is mostly also part Audio Engineer. EDM is the musical genre that truly shows the link between electronics and music because EDM Artists/Producers not only come up with a melody but also make the entire song, from the tiniest notes to the biggest synthesizers, all by themselves.
